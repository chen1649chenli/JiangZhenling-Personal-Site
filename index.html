Zhenling Jiang

Welcome to my webpage! I am a Ph.D. candidate in quantitative marketing at the John M. Olin School of Business at Washington University in St. Louis. My advisor is Professor Tat Y. Chan . I will be joining Georgia State University as an assistant professor in marketing.

Curriculum Vitae

Research Interest

I apply theory-driven empirical models to analyze various consumer behaviors. My job market paper studies how digit bias with numbers influences bargaining outcomes, with an application in the auto finance market. My research highlights agents’ bounded rationality in financial decision making, and investigates its impact on a large consumer purchase. I am also interested in studying consumer response to triggers or incentives using observational data. I have explored this topic in several contexts including loan uptake decisions when receiving a bonus payment, consumer retargeting from search history, and consumer retention decisions with loyalty program.

Industry Experience

My interest in understanding consumers started with my time at Bose Corporation, where I worked for more than five years prior to joining Olin. As a market researcher, I leveraged a variety of quantitative techniques to understand the "voice of the customer". For example, I built the company's first customer segmentation model that enabled a consumer centric discussion from engineering design to advertising strategy, and I use discrete choice models to elicit consumer trade-off among different attributes.
